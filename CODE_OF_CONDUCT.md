# Code of Conduct

## Our Commitment

The Wow community is committed to a respectful, inclusive, and technically constructive environment for contributors and users, regardless of experience, identity, background, or viewpoint.

## Expected Behavior

- Be respectful and patient in issues, reviews, discussions, and other project spaces.
- Focus disagreement on code, evidence, requirements, and trade-offs rather than people.
- Give actionable feedback and accept correction gracefully.
- Respect privacy and avoid sharing another person's confidential information.
- Help keep conversations relevant and welcoming to new contributors.

## Unacceptable Behavior

- Harassment, discrimination, threats, or personal attacks.
- Sexualized language or unwanted attention.
- Trolling, deliberate disruption, or repeated bad-faith argument.
- Publishing private information without permission.
- Any conduct that a reasonable person would consider hostile or unsafe in a professional community.

## Reporting and Enforcement

Report conduct concerns privately to [ahoowang@qq.com](mailto:ahoowang@qq.com). Include relevant links or screenshots and enough context to assess the incident. Do not use a public issue for sensitive reports.

Maintainers may edit or remove content, issue a warning, limit participation, or permanently exclude a participant when necessary. Reports will be reviewed as confidentially and impartially as practical, and retaliation against a reporter is not tolerated.

## Scope

This policy applies in project repositories, issue trackers, pull requests, documentation spaces, and public events or communication where someone represents the Wow project.
